STEMI

Unstable Angina

The time lapse from ACS diagnosis to Cath Lab presentation should guide which selection is indicated. To clarify: Because the definition is focused on when the patient "has" ACS (unstable angina, NSTEMI or STEMI), please use the diagnosis date/time - NCDR ACD#24910

⚠️ Reminder: A STEMI/NSTEMI which is diagnosed >7 days prior to the Cath Lab visit is considered 'over' by the registry and may no longer be captured as the Cath Lab or PCI indication. Please only code current symptoms/conditions prompting the Cath Lab visit – NCDR ACD# 25073
Angina
➔ Is the patient being taken to the Cath Lab for recent or ongoing chest pain, or on symptoms explicitly documented by the provider as an 'anginal equivalent'?

Does the patient have a history of:
• Prior MI
• Prior PCI
• CABG
or a history of angina that has increased in severity or frequency within the last 2 months?

Coronary Artery Disease History
➔ Does the patient have a history of CAD (Coronary Artery Disease)?

Is the patient CURRENTLY stable (no signs/symptoms of ACS, New Onset or Worsening Angina, or hemodynamic instability) AND has known CAD ≥50% in at least one vessel?

If the patient has a known history of CAD but is NOT currently stable, no selection here applies, move onto the next question
